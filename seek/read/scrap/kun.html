• The Desert

This mystery will not end
This search for truth is fraught with error
This precious walk between death and life
Is filled with both awe and terror

One blind corner
Leads to two blind alleys
By now you’d expect the grand finale

Yet here I am
Still awake
Conscious of every breath I take

You say that God does not exist
Yet I sincerely ask - then why do we?

A random blur
A spontaneous out growth
A grandiose cruel cosmic joke
A fiction penned by some unknown author

“The universe” - is all you offer

And so I give you the benefit of the doubt
And labor away in calculation
Fields, strings, groups, codes, spheres, loops, and all other contortions

“The universe” - is all you say
Then carry on your merry way
And I envy you in your self satisfaction
As I ponder this “principle of least action”

And all my friends have grown and cried
Yet here I remain - with this resounding “why?”

Why me or you?
Or the universe?
I demand an answer in a single verse!

“His command is only when He intends a thing that He says to it, “Be,” and it is.“
