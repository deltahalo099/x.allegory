CERTAINTY

Knowledge is easy
Certainty is hard

How do you know what you know is true?
And how do you know anything at all?

I can tell you a fact, but you’ll take it as that

Suppose you and a lover were standing on a shore and out in the distance were three lights flickering on and off.
First the left, then the middle, then the right. And you both wonder, what is that?
Are they related? Are they coincidence? Is it a single object? What’s the relationship between these lights?
You come up with a reasonably satisfactory explanation, but neither of you are entirely content with it.
The difference is one of you doesn’t stop thinking about it, while the other just moves on.

How are you to find out?
How are you to know with certainty?
You could devise a model and argue that it MUST be X,
because within your world of experience the only thing that comes close to these lights are X.
Given your limitations you’re unable to do much else to verify,
so you jot down your observations in a book and carry on.

A hundred years later someone stumbles on your book and is curious about the lights as well,
only that now they’re on a different side of the shore and someone has developed this magnificent device called a magnifying lens.
And so they direct this lens to the lights and observe a thread tied to each one,
based on this observation they conclude that you were only partially right,
but in truth these three lights are an aspect of one thing rather than three individual things.

Using this knowledge that person gets the idea to take a series of lenses and connect them with thread
(all of this is a fictional illustration of the nature of certainty)

And the process continues, each generation adding more to the wealth of observation and interpretation.
But never reaching the reality and essence of things.

The three lights may in reality be a strange sleeping creature with the ability to remain invisible,
never hunger, and at certain times of day there appears to be a thread connecting the lights.

As the collection and organization of sense experience “science” grows and the interrelations between the disparate areas are explored
it becomes more difficult to advance an idea - the idea must take into account all previously understood phenomena

But that does not preclude the possibility that everyone may indeed be completely wrong…
You’ll just have to figure it out and try harder

This is the method of science.


When it comes to history the possibility of experimentation are gone,
one has to rely on textual sources, human memory, video evidence, archeological evidence,
and the method of explanation and verification can never be as certain.


How is one to be certain and to know anything without a doubt then?

CAUSALITY

The notion of chance/randomness/probability. Do they have their own reality, or are they a matter of limitation regarding our knowledge?

The best example is the rolling of dice.

Someone will say “the outcome is up to chance” - but from a deterministic physics view the die is a complex mechanical system.
All the subtle weight distribution, all the precise collisions, the position and momentum of the hand at the moment of release.
The rotations for the right duration of time. We can even include the subtle electromagnetic forces between the air particles
and the die as it moves through the air.

In this sense there is no “chance” - just the way it MUST be given the circumstances.
The outcome could be any of 6. But when I get a specific value. There’s no way it could have been another.
Almost as if it were destined for that outcome to occur. Destined from the very moment the die was released from the hand.

The question then, is it relevant or meaningful to then talk about what COULD have happened?
It COULD have been a different number, but then in that reality it would have been destined just the same…

We now extend this to the “dice roll of the universe” - from the very first instant,
to the formation and explosion of stars, to the formation and accumulation of raw metals to form the earth to be positioned
just the precise distance from the sun for water and life to have the possibility to emerge, to then have the right circumstances
for amino acids to form to then have the process of natural selection arise to have the creatures evolve to be in the precise environment
to make the right adaptations to lead to the decisions that led to further adaptation that led to certain civilizations which
led to certain innovations and a certain pair of people to birth me to write this and you to read it.

We’ll call that outcome “3”

We could then naively ask “how did all of this arise?”

And someone will respond “just chance…”

After all, it COULD have been “5” and not “3,” but it’s not…

This is intimately linked to the nature of the atom, because the die is composed of this fundamental substrate
- whatever is at the very bottom - and it must be the interactions of these fundamentals that ultimately govern the behavior of the whole event.

Which takes us to quantum mechanics and quantum field theory. Frankly I don’t know enough here to make any accurate statements,
other than the fact that currently “particles” are no longer “points” in space, but rather probability distributions in a general cloud.
And even farther still “disturbances in an all pervading field”

What the that means I don’t know. How does this effect our argument?
